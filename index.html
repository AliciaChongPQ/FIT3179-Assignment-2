Water-Borne Disease Incidence in Malaysia (2018-2021):

Trends and Public Health Impact

Throughout Recent Years

TYPHOID: bacterium Salmonella typhi. It primarily spreads through ingestion of food or water that has been contaminated by feces from an infected person.

The disease presents with high fever, fatigue, headache, abdominal pain, and diarrhea or constipation. A distinctive rose-colored rash may also appear in some cases.

HEPATITIS A: caused by the Hepatitis A virus (HAV), which primarily spreads through consumption of contaminated water or food, particularly in regions with poor sanitation. The virus affects the liver.

Symptoms include fever, fatigue, nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and jaundice (yellowing of the skin and eyes). Hepatitis A is typically less severe than other forms of hepatitis but can still cause significant illness.

Who is Most Impacted?

Immunity in Endemic Areas:

In regions where Hepatitis A is endemic, many individuals may acquire the virus in childhood, leading to the development of lifelong immunity. As Hepatitis A tends to cause milder symptoms in children, they often recover without complications. Typhoid, on the other hand, can affect individuals of all ages and does not result in the same kind of widespread, naturally acquired immunity.

Transmission Routes:

While both diseases are transmitted through the fecal-oral route, Typhoid tends to be more persistent in environments with poor sanitation. Typhoid is caused by Salmonella typhi, which can survive longer in contaminated water and food. Hepatitis A virus (HAV), while also transmitted through contaminated food and water, does not persist as long in the environment compared to Typhoid bacteria.

Water quality complaints may arise from unsafe drinking water, which could contain harmful pathogens, including those that cause typhoid. Poor water treatment infrastructure, unclean water distribution systems, or inadequate filtering can contribute to contamination.

When water supply is interrupted, people resort to unsafe alternative water sources, increasing their exposure to contaminated water. Interruption in water supply can also disrupt hygiene practices, such as regular handwashing, which is critical in preventing the spread of diseases.

TYPHOID:

Children are particularly vulnerable to typhoid because of weaker immune systems. They often face more severe symptoms like high fever, diarrhea, and potential complications if not treated promptly.

Adults may have partial immunity due to prior exposure to the disease, especially in endemic areas, leading to milder symptoms.

Elderlies may suffer more severe complications due to underlying health conditions and weakened immune responses.

HEPATITIS A:

Children often have milder or asymptomatic cases of hepatitis A, with many not showing symptoms at all. Despite this, they are efficient spreaders of the virus, as they may unknowingly transmit it to others.

Adults are more likely to experience severe symptoms. Jaundice, extreme fatigue, and liver inflammation are more common.

Elderlies may experience a longer recovery time and are more prone to complications such as liver failure.

How Do External Factors Play a Role?

Areas without access to clean water are more vulnerable to outbreaks of water-borne diseases during floods, monsoon seasons, or other environmental conditions that exacerbate the contamination of water sources.

In such regions, people may also have difficulty washing produce or cooking utensils. This can contribute to the ingestion of harmful pathogens, leading to a higher infection rates.

Water Treatment Plant Density has minimal correlation with the incidence rates of these diseases. This may be due to the fact that the quality and maintenance of these infrastructures play a bigger role.

Additionally, behaviorial factors may hold a bigger impact. Even with improved water treatment, if individuals continue to engage in practices that contribute to disease transmission (e.g., poor sanitation, or improper storage of water), the expected decrease in disease rates may not materialize.
